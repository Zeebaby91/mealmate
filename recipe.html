Jollof Rice

A vibrant, smoky West African rice dish cooked with tomatoes, peppers, and spices, loved for its rich flavor and festive aroma.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups rice
  • 4 tomatoes
  • 2 red peppers
  • 2 onions
  • Seasonings & oil

Steps:

  1. Wash the rice and set aside.
  2. Blend tomatoes, peppers, and onions into a sauce.
  3. Fry sauce in oil, add the pepper mixture.
  4. Add seasonings and stock.
  5. Add rice and cook until soft.

Fried Rice

A colorful rice dish stir-fried with vegetables, liver or chicken, and spices, often served at parties and celebrations.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups rice
  • Sliced carrots, peas, green pepper, and sweet corn
  • Vegetable oil
  • Liver or chicken and chicken stock
  • Seasonings

Steps:

  1. Parboil rice and set aside.
  2. Stir-fry vegetables in oil.
  3. Add liver/chicken and seasoning.
  4. Mix rice with the vegetables and fry together.

Pounded Yam with Egusi Soup

A classic Nigerian delicacy — soft, stretchy yam paired with rich melon seed soup cooked with palm oil, meat, and vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • Yam
  • Egusi (melon seeds)
  • Palm oil
  • Vegetables
  • Meat or fish
  • Seasonings

Steps:

  1. Boil yam until soft, then pound until smooth.
  2. Fry ground egusi with palm oil and onions.
  3. Add meat, seasonings, stock, and vegetables.
  4. Serve hot with pounded yam.

Suya (Spicy Grilled Meat)

Thinly sliced, skewered beef grilled with yaji spice mix, a street food favorite with a smoky, spicy kick.

Ingredients:

  • 500g beef (thinly sliced)
  • Suya spice mix (ground peanuts, cayenne pepper, ginger, garlic, paprika)
  • Salt & seasoning cubes
  • Vegetable oil
  • Onions & tomatoes (for garnish)

Steps:

  1. Slice beef into thin strips.
  2. Marinate with oil and suya spice mix for at least 1 hour.
  3. Skewer the beef and grill until cooked.
  4. Garnish with sliced onions, tomatoes, and cabbage.
  5. Serve hot with flatbread or alone.

Moi Moi (Steamed Bean Pudding)

A soft and savory pudding made from blended beans, peppers, onions, and oil, often served with rice or eaten alone.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups peeled beans (black-eyed peas)
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers
  • 1 onion
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • Seasoning cubes & salt
  • Boiled egg or fish (optional)

Steps:

  1. Blend beans, peppers, and onion into a smooth paste.
  2. Add oil, salt, and seasoning cubes. Mix well.
  3. Pour into foil/nylon wraps or ramekins.
  4. Add sliced boiled egg or fish if desired.
  5. Steam for about 40–60 minutes until firm.

Pepper Soup (Spicy Broth)

A hot, spicy broth made with goat meat or fish and flavored with traditional pepper soup spices, perfect for cold evenings.

Ingredients:

  • Pepper Soup (Spicy Broth)
  • Pepper soup spice mix
  • Scotch bonnet peppers
  • Onions
  • Seasoning cubes & salt
  • Scent leaves (optional)
  • meat/fish/chicken

Steps:

  1. Wash meat/fish and boil with onions, seasoning, and salt.
  2. Add pepper soup spice and blended peppers.
  3. Cook until meat is tender.
  4. Add scent leaves for aroma.
  5. Serve hot with rice, yam, or alone.
